To Internet Providers and Operators

VK Peering Policy
This document is not a contract or public offer. This is an instruction for internet providers and operators describing how to establish peering (direct traffic exchange) with VK. Permanent link to this document: http://vk.com/help.php?page=peering

Peering via Public Internet Exchanges
The easiest and fastest way to establish peering with VK network is to connect to Internet Exchange (IX) where VK is also present. VK freely exchanges traffic via all route servers through all connected IX with all operators present there without any additional conditions or restrictions. We try to avoid private BGP sessions whenever traffic exchange through route servers is possible.

Direct Peering
Another possibility to peer with VK network is to organize a direct connection. Since our equipment is located in St. Petersburg (Russia), direct peering is possible only there. For fault-tolerance and load-balancing purposes we will ask you to connect simultaneously to two destinations: data centers on 21 Tsvetochnaya str. and 57 Borovaya str.

While selecting peering partners for direct peering connections we check the amount of traffic generated between our AS-SET, it should be at least 1,5-2% of our total traffic (that is at least 15-20 Gbit/s). We usually do not establish peering connections with clients of already existing peering partners except cases when existing traffic route does not provide a good site access speed for end users.

When several routes to end-user are available, we usually (but not necessarily) set Local Preference for peer BGP sessions proportional to available free link capacity (the more the capacity, the higher is the Local Preference). Please pay attention that attempts to override our preferences by artificial splitting of announced prefixes (more specific) will lead to peering deactivation.
